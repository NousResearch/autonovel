## The Logic of Governance & Community Trust
*(Functional equivalent of the magic system — explicitly identified as such in world.md)*

- The ARB (Architectural Review Board) reviews any exterior change to a property within Haymarket's historic district, including signage, paint colors, visible landscaping, and structural modifications. (world.md)
- The ARB meets monthly. (world.md)
- The ARB can approve, deny, or table a submission; tabling on first review is the most common outcome. (world.md)
- The cost of ARB approval is always time, and the willingness to revise. (world.md)
- The Planning Commission reviews land use changes, special use permits, and changes to what a property is used for. (world.md)
- The Planning Commission makes recommendations; the Town Council makes final decisions. (world.md)
- A Pup's Valley required a special use permit because a dog daycare is not a standard commercial use. (world.md)
- The Town Council has five elected members. (world.md)
- The Town Council meets twice monthly in regular session and can call special meetings. (world.md)
- Citizens Time is a standing agenda item at Town Council meetings; any resident may speak for three minutes. (world.md)
- The three-minute limit at Citizens Time is enforced with courtesy but is enforced. (world.md)
- Everything said at a Town Council meeting is entered into the minutes by the Town Clerk. (world.md)
- Nothing in Haymarket's approval process happens fast; this is described as a feature, not dysfunction. (world.md)
- Community trust in Haymarket is accrued slowly and spent quickly. (world.md)
- Trust transfers between community members but not completely — an endorsement is an introduction, not a guarantee. (world.md)
- The cost of trust violation in Haymarket is described as permanent — there is no rebranding after a betrayal. (world.md)
- The strategy for navigating Haymarket's approval process is to show up before you need anything — to be known before you are an applicant. (world.md)
- Dottie Leonard's endorsement of the Paynes opened doors they still had to walk through themselves. (world.md, characters.md)
- The historical overlay (brick sidewalks, lamp posts, ARB standards) was formalized in the late 1990s–2000s; Dottie Leonard's influence is on this framework. (world.md)
- The BPOL (Business, Professional, and Occupational License) tax is a real municipal business license fee in Haymarket. (seed.txt, world.md)
