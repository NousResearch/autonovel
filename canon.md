## THE LOGIC OF THE WORLD
*(Nonfiction equivalent of magic system rules — governing what is possible, what it costs, what happens when broken)*

### Logic One: The Logic of Small Business

- Rule 1: Trust is the product. A Pup's Valley sells the certainty that the most important non-human member of someone's family will be safe, happy, and known by name. All other services are downstream of this. (WORLD.MD)
- Breaking trust once, publicly, ends the business. This is treated as a physical law of the industry, not a risk. (WORLD.MD)
- Rule 2: In a town of Haymarket's size, word of mouth is not a marketing channel — it is the only channel. (WORLD.MD)
- Rule 3: Fixed costs (rent, utilities, payroll, insurance) continue when revenue stops. The gap between fixed costs and zero revenue is the definition of an existential crisis. (WORLD.MD)
- The BPOL 30-day extension approved April 14, 2020 was a deferral, not a solution — it did not change the math. (WORLD.MD)
- The cost of building a small business is not primarily financial — it is the cost of sustained attention: every dog's name, every family's story, every permit deadline, every council meeting, every review. (WORLD.MD)
- A small business in a small town cannot be anonymous. The Paynes could not have a bad day that no one noticed. (WORLD.MD)
- The limitation of total accountability is also the business's greatest asset: total accountability means total connection. (WORLD.MD)

### Logic Two: The Logic of Community

- Citizens Time is the portion of a Town Council meeting where residents can walk to the microphone and speak for three minutes about anything. (WORLD.MD)
- The Haymarket Town Council has five members. (WORLD.MD)
- The Dottie Principle: Dottie's support for something functioned as a signal to other long-term residents because she had been there long enough and done enough real work that her judgment was a community resource. (WORLD.MD)
- Community membership requires showing up — to meetings, to funerals, to conversations without agendas. (WORLD.MD)
- The question "what does Dottie think?" carried weight that no advertising budget could replicate. (WORLD.MD)

### Logic Three: The Logic of Dogs

- Dogs reflect emotional reality without editing it. A nervous dog in a new facility is a direct readout of whether the facility is safe. (WORLD.MD)
- Dogs cannot be charmed, fooled by presentation, or bought with marketing language. (WORLD.MD)
- A dog that runs to Jim when it sees him is a direct readout of whether Jim is who he appears to be. (WORLD.MD)
- A cage-free dog daycare is a managed social environment, not a kennel. Running it well requires continuous attention, genuine expertise, and the ability to read a room full of dogs. (WORLD.MD)
- The cost of getting it wrong in a cage-free facility is immediate and visible — there is no hiding a bad day. (WORLD.MD)
- Dogs cannot tell you what happened to them before they arrived. Jim can only pay attention to what the dog shows him and respond to that. (WORLD.MD)
- The limitation — the gap between what a dog has experienced and what it can communicate — is described as one of the story's quiet themes. (WORLD.MD)
