### Zoning and Permitting (Hard Rules)

- Exterior signage in Haymarket requires approval; size, color, font, and placement are all subject to review. (world.md)
- A change of use — converting a commercial space to a different kind of commercial use — triggers a new permit process. (world.md)
- A new permit process triggers public notice, which triggers public comment. (world.md)
- The historical overlay governs the appearance of anything visible from the street in Haymarket; this is not advisory. (world.md)
- Every permit process takes weeks or months. (world.md)
- Haymarket operates under Prince William County zoning law, overlaid with its own municipal regulations and the historical overlay. (world.md)
- BPOL is a real tax instrument applicable to Haymarket businesses. (seed.txt, world.md)
- A 30-day BPOL extension was the concrete relief the Town Council was able to provide in April 2020. (seed.txt, world.md)

### Community Memory (Soft Law)

- Community memory in a small town has weight in every decision, functioning as an informal but real system of power. (world.md)
- Dottie Leonard did not have a formal role in the Paynes' approval process; her influence was through fifty years of accumulated trust. (world.md)
- Jim knew the names of the councilmembers, the town planner, and the police chief before he needed to know them. (seed.txt, world.md)
- Showing up at meetings before you need anything is described as both good citizenship and strategy. (world.md)
- Community memory cannot be bought or borrowed; it must be earned in person, over time, by showing up. (world.md)

### Pandemic Economics

- The COVID-19 shutdown removed the conditions under which A Pup's Valley existed as a business. (world.md)
- When the Governor's shutdown order was issued, A Pup's Valley's revenue did not slow — it stopped. (world.md)
- Dog daycare requires dogs to be away from home; when people were home, the business model was suspended. (world.md)
- There was no internal business workaround for the shutdown; the Paynes could not pivot to a different model. (world.md)

## Discrepancies & Notes

- **Dottie's age at death vs. years in Haymarket:** Dottie died at 75 (March 20, 2024) and lived in Haymarket for "over fifty years." This is internally consistent — she would have arrived in Haymarket around age 24 or younger — but no source specifies when exactly she arrived. Writers should not specify an arrival date without additional sourcing.
- **Andy's age:** Characters.md describes Andy as "approximately elementary school age" during 2018–2019. No source gives a specific age or birth year. Writers should not assign a specific age without additional sourcing.
- **Jim and Andrea's prior facility:** Both seed.txt and world.md confirm they ran a prior award-winning pet care facility, but neither document names it, gives its location, or specifies when they ran it or why they left. Writers should not invent these details.
- **Dottie's Council tenure:** Seed.txt states she "served on the real Town Council." World.md confirms she was not a councilmember at the time of the Paynes' approval process. The specific dates of her Council service are not given in any source document.
- **The Disney's America fight:** Seed.txt describes Dottie as "instrumental in stopping" it. World.md confirms she was "in those rooms" and describes her role as one among others ("she and others had argued about Disney's America, and won"). Writers should not overstate her role as sole or primary architect of the opposition.
- **Andrea's podium appearance:** Seed.txt states Mayor Leake "stops a budget discussion to invite Andrea Payne to the podium." World.md confirms this. Neither document specifies whether Andrea had requested to speak before being invited, though characters.md notes she "had something to say before he asked." This is a characterization, not a confirmed logistical fact.
