## Rules of This World
*(Reframed from "Magic System Rules" — these are the hard rules of Haymarket's civic and social reality)*

- **Rule One:** Everything is public record. Every Town Council meeting is recorded in minutes; every Citizens Time comment, vote, motion, and amendment is documented permanently. (WORLD.MD)
- Showing up to a public meeting is a permanent act — it cannot be undone or erased from the record. (WORLD.MD)
- Opposition and mistakes made at public meetings are also permanently on record. (WORLD.MD)
- **Rule Two:** Approval requires trust, not just legal compliance. A permit applicant can meet every legal requirement and still not receive approval, because approval in a small town is a social act as much as a legal one. (WORLD.MD)
- The Town Planner can find reasons to delay an application. (WORLD.MD)
- The ARB can request revisions to an application. (WORLD.MD)
- Neighbors can show up and object to an application at a public hearing. (WORLD.MD)
- Trust cannot be rushed or bought; it can only be accumulated slowly through presence and reliability over time. (WORLD.MD)
- Being a resident before being a business owner is a meaningful sequence that affects how trust is extended. (WORLD.MD)
- **Rule Three (Dottie Leonard's Law):** In a small town, certain individuals hold structural social weight whose endorsement opens rooms and whose skepticism creates significant costs, independent of any formal office they hold. (WORLD.MD)
- This kind of endorsement is earned by the person who gives it, not manufactured by the person who receives it. (WORLD.MD)
- **Rule Four:** External catastrophe (e.g., a pandemic shutdown) is not personal but lands personally; it does not grade on effort or preparation. (WORLD.MD)
- Survival after catastrophe requires a specific stubbornness independent of talent or preparation. (WORLD.MD)
- **Rule Five:** Dogs are honest. A dog's response in any scene is the accurate reading of the situation. Dogs do not hedge, manage expectations, or lie. (WORLD.MD, CHARACTERS.MD)
- Rule Five carries no cost — it is described as "the one rule with no cost" and "the gift at the center of the story." (WORLD.MD)
- Buildings in the historic district of Haymarket cannot be significantly altered without ARB approval. (WORLD.MD)
- The ARB is the body responsible for maintaining the historical overlay. (WORLD.MD)
- BPOL refers to a business license payment mechanism subject to Town Council deadlines. (SEED.TXT — referenced in April 14, 2020 meeting context)
